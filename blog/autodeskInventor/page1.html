    Safety is a huge hurdle for many educators as they enter into the design world. One of the common comments I hear is, "I feel fine using the tools, but I don't trust the students to use them properly." We, as teachers, have to remember that we are in a position to (drum roll please...) teach our students. It is an honorable position to be in, and a top reason why we are in this profession.

    Schools are supposed to be a place that a student can make a mistake and it is okay, because they have a knowledgable and capable adult by their side to give them guidance. Obviously, no one wants a student to be harmed. However, by restricting the use of a tool to just a teacher, we are a disservice to our top clients and their learning. Instead, we can teach the students the proper usage, proper technique, and proper awareness needed to successfully operate the tool.

    You, as the teacher, should know that it is okay to share with your students your hesitations and expectations. Honesty and forthcoming will provide a common understanding for the betterment of your classroom.
